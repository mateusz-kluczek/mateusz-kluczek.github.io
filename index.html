About Me

Who Am I?

Hi, I'm Mateusz Kluczek I graduated from University College Cork (UCC) with PhD degree in applied mathematics. My research describes nonlinear wave-current interactions in the nearshore. I studied mathematical aspects of geophysical flows on the Earth.

The geophysical fluid dynamics is study of naturally occuring and large-scale flows mostly on Earth. The main characteristic of geophysical flow are the effects of rotation and stratification. The project was funded by Science Foundation of Ireland.

Afterwards, I was working at Technion as a postdoc. My work was still concerned with nonlinear wave interactions, but I focus on probability characteristics of rogue waves. Rouge waves are phenomenon occuring on the oceans where the interactions between different type of waves is a cause of an occurence of a wave with unexpectedly high amplitude.

Nowdays, I expand my carrier in the high tech sector where I can utilise all the expirience and skills build at academia.
