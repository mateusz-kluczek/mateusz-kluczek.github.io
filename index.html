About Me

Who Am I?

Hi I'm Mateusz Kluczek I am doing a PhD in Applied Math at University College Cork. My research work is related to nonlinear wave-current interactions in the nearshore. I study the mathematical aspects of geophysical flows on the Earth.

The geophysical fluid dynamics is the study of naturally occuring and large-scale flows mostly on Earth. The main characteristic of geophysical flow are the effects of rotation and stratification. The project is funded by Science Foundation of Ireland.

What I do?

Here are some of my expertise

Nonlinear waves

Geophysical water waves governed by Euler equations incorporating the Coriolis force

Dispersion relation

The relation defining the phase speed of the wave for a fixed physical parameters of a fluid flow

Exact solutions

Existence of exact and explicit solutions for a different type of a wave motion in oceans

Internal water waves

The exact and explicit solutions for the governing equations describing nonlinear internal water waves

Currents

Study of solutions including depth-invariant underlying currents


Financial Math

A master degree in financial mathematics and experience in working for international financial institutions
